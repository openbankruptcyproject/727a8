How This Calculator Works

The calculator applies the following rules from the Bankruptcy Code:

  • Prior Chapter 7 or 11 discharge: You must wait 8 years from the prior filing date to file a new Chapter 7 and receive a discharge. This is Section 727(a)(8).
  • Prior Chapter 13 discharge: You must wait 6 years from the prior filing date -- unless you paid 100% of unsecured claims, or paid at least 70% in good faith with best effort. This is Section 727(a)(9).
  • Prior Chapter 12 discharge: Same 6-year rule as Chapter 13, with the same exceptions. Also Section 727(a)(9).

All periods are measured from filing date to filing date. The discharge date of the prior case does not affect the calculation.

What About Filing Chapter 13 Instead?

This calculator only addresses Chapter 7 eligibility. If you want to file Chapter 13 instead, different rules apply under Section 1328(f):

  • Prior Chapter 7 discharge: 4-year bar on Chapter 13 discharge (filing date to filing date)
  • Prior Chapter 13 discharge: 2-year bar on Chapter 13 discharge (filing date to filing date)
