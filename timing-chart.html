The 8-Year Discharge Bar: Visual Timeline

Section 727(a)(8) creates an 8-year waiting period between Chapter 7 filings. The clock starts on the date you filed your prior case -- not the date you received your discharge. Here is how the timeline works.

Example: Filed Chapter 7 on March 15, 2018

Filed: 3/15/2018
Eligible: 3/15/2026

8 years, filing date to filing date. Discharge date (6/20/2018) is irrelevant.

All Discharge Bar Timelines

Section 727(a)(8) is just one of several time-based bars. Here is how they all compare:

Prior CaseNew CaseWaitStatuteExceptions?
Ch. 7Ch. 78 years727(a)(8)None
Ch. 13Ch. 76 years727(a)(9)Yes (100% or 70%+good faith)
Ch. 7Ch. 134 years1328(f)(1)None
Ch. 13Ch. 132 years1328(f)(2)None
Ch. 11Ch. 78 years727(a)(8)None
Ch. 12Ch. 76 years727(a)(9)Yes (same as Ch. 13)

Key Point: All periods are filing date to filing date

Every one of these waiting periods is measured from the date the prior petition was filed to the date the new petition is filed. The discharge date never matters for this calculation.
