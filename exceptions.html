Section 727(a)(8) -- Exceptions

Are There Exceptions to the 8-Year Bar?

No. Section 727(a)(8) has no exceptions.

Unlike Section 727(a)(9), which allows exceptions when the debtor paid 100% of unsecured claims or paid at least 70% in good faith with best effort, Section 727(a)(8) is absolute. If you received a Chapter 7 or Chapter 11 discharge in a case filed within 8 years of your new filing, your discharge will be denied. No court has the discretion to waive this requirement.

This is one of the clearest rules in the Bankruptcy Code. The statute says "the court shall grant the debtor a discharge, unless" the prior discharge was in a case "commenced within 8 years." The word "shall" combined with "unless" leaves no room for judicial discretion.

Why 727(a)(8) Has No Exceptions (But 727(a)(9) Does)

The logic is straightforward:

  • 727(a)(8) applies when your prior case was Chapter 7 -- a full liquidation where debts were eliminated without repayment. Congress decided that 8 years is the minimum wait before another full discharge.
  • 727(a)(9) applies when your prior case was Chapter 13 or Chapter 12 -- repayment plans where you paid creditors back. Because you made an effort to repay, Congress created exceptions for cases where you paid a significant amount.

The 727(a)(9) exceptions are:

  1. You paid 100% of unsecured claims in the prior Chapter 13/12 plan, OR
  2. You paid at least 70% of unsecured claims AND the plan was proposed in good faith with best effort

Neither exception applies to 727(a)(8). If your prior discharge was Chapter 7, the 8-year bar is absolute.

What To Do Instead

If you are within the 8-year window and need debt relief, here are your options:

Option 1: Wait

The simplest approach. Use our date calculator to find your exact eligibility date. If you are close, waiting may be the best strategy.

Option 2: File Chapter 13

The 727(a)(8) bar only applies to Chapter 7 discharges. You can file Chapter 13 at any time. However, Section 1328(f)(1) bars a Chapter 13 discharge if you file within 4 years of a prior Chapter 7 filing. Even without a discharge, Chapter 13 can provide the automatic stay and a structured repayment plan.

Option 3: Non-bankruptcy alternatives

  • Debt negotiation: Many creditors will settle for less than the full balance, especially if the debt is old
  • Debt management plan: A nonprofit credit counseling agency can negotiate lower interest rates and consolidated payments
  • Statute of limitations: Some old debts may be unenforceable if the statute of limitations has expired
  • Judgment-proof status: If you have no assets and exempt income, creditors may have no practical way to collect
